About me

My name is Sasha Fleischman. Currently, I am a college student studying at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology in Cambridge, Massachusetts. As a high schooler, I attended Maybeck High School, a small private high school in Berkeley.

When people first meet me, I strike them as an social recluse who would do anything to shy away from the limelight. But the truth is, when I get to know a person really well, I can't stop talking.

As a victim of a hate crime, I advocate for equality everywhere I go. I believe that people should not be treated harshly just because of their personal opinions.

The Fire

A self-reflection of the worst event of my life.

Life before the fire

Before the fire, my life was normal. I had friends, I went to a great school, and I had parents that loved me. Even after coming out as agender, I did not have any significant "moments" where I felt discriminated.



The day of the fire

I remember walking to the BART Station with Michael before I got on the bus. When I got on the bus, I found a seat in the back and drifted asleep immediately. When my skirt was set in flames, I could not think. My heart rate skyrocketed, and all I could do was panic.


Hospital

In the beginning, I was extremely anxious and I couldn't concentrate. After my first surgery, things started to set into a routine. I didn't have the energy to do anything productive, and my favorite part of the day was being bathed in the tub. I went through a lot of pain during my time in the hospital, but in the end, I was finally free.

Transition to daily life

When I got home, the reporters just swarmed around everywhere, looking for an interview. On top of that, my legs went through the side-effects of surgery which included pain and itching. Simple daily tasks were hard to complete. The only thing that brought me joy was being around my friends and family again.

Justice

I felt like Richard should have been forgiven and punished at the same time. But, I genuinely didn't want him to be tried as an adult. I guess I was kind of happy because of the plea deal because he would serve less time, but at the same time he would still learn his lesson.

My reflection on everything so far

After everything that has happened, I have moved on from it. I have learned that the best way to forgive, is to forget. The fire is now a distant memory and I intend to keep it that way. After college, I aspire to educate more people about gender and sexuality.
